Permeable Pavers Contractor

Permeable pavers appeal to homeowners who wish to reduce the environmental impact of hardscaping.

The installation of permeable pavers is intended to allow water to drain slowly through the base layers. This method of installing pavers is known as "open grading." We use larger gravel pieces and more "open" seams to facilitate the flow of water through the soil. The water is exposed to fewer potentially hazardous contaminants, which reduces harmful runoff and protects watersheds.

While permeable pavers are typically a bit more expensive to install, they typically require less time. Typically, a skilled Napa paver contractor can complete the installation quickly, allowing you to immediately begin enjoying your outdoor space.

In addition, they do not require a separate drainage system, making them comparable in price to standard concrete pavers. Permeable pavers are an excellent, eco-friendly option for hardscaping projects such as installing a patio, a pool deck, or a walkway.
