Sonoma Permeable Pavers Contractor

Permeable pavers are low-maintenance and require less upkeep than traditional “hard” surfaces. Without the accumulation of runoff, there is no need for frequent cleaning or maintenance. The water that passes through permeable pavers also helps to aerate and fertilize the soil beneath, making it more healthy and resistant to erosion.

We install permeable pavers utilizing a technique known as “open grading”. This involves laying larger gravel or using other material pieces in between, creating spaces that allow water to pass through. This keeps the water on-site, reducing runoff and improving water quality. The permeable pavers are also more eco-friendly than traditional hardscaping materials as they reduce the need for stormwater management systems, and help to conserve soil moisture.

Overall, permeable pavers offer an attractive way to reduce environmental impacts from hardscaping and protect watersheds. They are more expensive to install initially than traditional hardscaping materials, but they require less maintenance over time and can stand up better to extreme weather conditions while still looking great. They also reduce the need for installing drainage features.
