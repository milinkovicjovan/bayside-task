North Bay Permeable Pavers Contractor

Permeable pavers are designed to be more eco-friendly than traditional hardscaping, as they allow water to drain through the base layers and back into the ground. This helps reduce soil erosion and contamination of surface waters while also restoring natural groundwater reserves.

The installation process for permeable pavers is different from traditional paver installations, as it involves using larger gravel pieces and more "open" seams to facilitate the flow of water through the soil.

Although permeable paver installation is usually more expensive than traditional pavers, they can actually save money in the long run due to their durability and low maintenance. Permeable pavers are designed to be very resistant to cracking, fading, and staining, so they will look great for years after installation. Additionally, since water can pass through them, there is no need for costly sealants or treatments that are often required with traditional paver installations.

Installing permeable pavers is relatively straightforward compared to conventional pavers. Generally, an experienced North Bay paver contractor can complete the installation quickly, allowing you to begin enjoying your outdoor space without delay.
